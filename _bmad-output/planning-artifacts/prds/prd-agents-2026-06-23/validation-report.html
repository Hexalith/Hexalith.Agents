Fair

The PRD has a strong product spine: named AI participant identity, explicit conversation-originated invocation, confirmation workflow, tenant isolation, and audit are coherent and consistently reinforced.

It is usable for UX and architecture discovery, but it is not yet safe as a direct story-generation or launch-readiness source because two core launch behaviors remain under-specified: how Conversation Context is bounded when it exceeds model capacity, and what safety/content policy gates generated responses before automatic posting or proposal creation.

Decision-readiness

adequate

The document makes real decisions instead of hiding behind generic AI-assistant language. The Vision commits to named, governed AI participants and explicitly excludes long-term memory, tools, project content, folder content, external channels, and ambient triggers.

The risk is that several questions are labeled non-blocking for PRD finalization while they are blocking for implementation or release readiness. That is acceptable only if downstream consumers treat revisit conditions as gates.

high

Safety policy is deferred even though generated content can be posted automatically

section 7; section 12 OQ-9
The actual AI response safety policy is only an open question, while Automatic Response Mode can turn generated output into a Conversation Message without human approval.
Fix: Add a Content Safety / Prompt Policy NFR and block generation or posting when policy checks fail.
medium

Launch thresholds are still placeholders

section 11 SM-2, SM-3; section 12 OQ-11
The adoption and approval metrics do not define numerator, denominator, target, time window, or launch cohort.
Fix: Add required metric fields and make threshold selection a launch-readiness gate.

Substance over theater

adequate

Most sections earn their place. The personas are tied to workflows, the non-goals protect the V1 bet, and audit/security requirements are product-specific. Performance and Cost Control are still assumption statements rather than testable requirements.

medium

Performance and cost control are not yet product requirements

section 7; section 12 OQ-5, OQ-6
Latency and cost constraints influence automatic replies, confirmation workflow, context size, provider/model selection, and tenant quotas.
Fix: Add minimum product-level constraints for status visibility, quota/budget decisions, and production enablement.

Strategic coherence

strong

The thesis is clear and repeated without becoming vague: Hexalith Agents proves governed participation inside Hexalith Conversations, not a general autonomous-agent platform. The feature set follows that thesis.

Done-ness clarity

thin

Most FRs include testable consequences, and the proposal workflow is concrete. The thinness is concentrated in generation behavior: Conversation Context construction and AI safety checks are not yet expressed as done criteria.

high

The full Source Conversation context requirement is not implementable as written for oversized conversations

section 4.4 FR-9; section 6.1; section 12 OQ-10
The PRD promises full context but later asks how context should be bounded when too large for the selected model.
Fix: Replace the absolute promise with a Conversation Context Policy: complete context when it fits, deterministic fail-closed or approved bounded behavior when it does not, no silent truncation, and context policy/version in Audit Evidence.
medium

Proposal notification remains below story-ready detail

section 4.6 FR-13; section 12 OQ-4
Approver discovery is specified, but the notification path is still unresolved.
Fix: Define where pending proposals appear, what indicators are required, and whether launch requires active notification or only an in-product queue.
low

Safe to disclose is not testable

section 4.3 FR-7; section 4.9 FR-24/FR-25
The PRD does not define disclosure categories for approval-policy basis values.
Fix: Define values that are always displayable, operator-only, redacted, or omitted from external API responses.

Scope honesty

strong

The PRD is honest about boundaries. V1 non-goals are explicit, V2 items are kept out of scope, assumptions are indexed, and unresolved questions have owners plus revisit conditions.

medium

Generated-content retention and deletion behavior is deferred even though audit storage is in MVP scope

section 9; section 12 OQ-8
Generated AI drafts can contain sensitive conversation content and will be stored outside the Conversation until approved or terminal.
Fix: Explicitly bind Agent audit records to the existing platform policy by name, or block audit-storage stories until OQ-8 is resolved.

Downstream usability

adequate

The artifact is easy to source-extract. Glossary terms are present, FR IDs are contiguous, UJ IDs are clear, and the public-surface section gives architecture and API work a useful boundary. The remaining blockers are the unresolved context, safety, notification, and retention decisions already called out above.

Shape fit

strong

The shape fits a launch-level, multi-stakeholder B2B/platform capability. User journeys with named protagonists are useful because administration, runtime calling, approval, and API integration are distinct workflows.
