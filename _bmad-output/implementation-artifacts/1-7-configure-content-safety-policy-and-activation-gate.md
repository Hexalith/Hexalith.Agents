# Story 1.7: Configure Content Safety Policy And Activation Gate

Status: done

<!-- Note: Validation is optional. Run validate-create-story for quality check before dev-story. -->

## Story

As an Agent Administrator or release operator,
I want to configure the active Content Safety and prompt policy for `hexa`,
so that production or production-like enablement cannot proceed without explicit safety rules.

## Acceptance Criteria

**AC1 - Defining a Content Safety Policy records the safe policy fields + a policy version, auditable and future-only**
**Given** `hexa` has identity, Provider/model, response mode, and policy configuration
**When** an authorized administrator or release operator defines Content Safety Policy
**Then** the policy records prompt constraints, blocked or restricted output categories, safety failure handling, audit treatment, and policy version
**And** changes are auditable and affect only future Agent Calls.

**AC2 - Activation fails closed when no active Content Safety Policy exists, without exposing unsafe policy content**
**Given** no active Content Safety Policy exists
**When** an administrator attempts to activate `hexa` for production or production-like launch validation
**Then** activation is rejected
**And** status identifies Content Safety Policy as a blocker without exposing unsafe policy content.

**AC3 - Both modes share the active policy unless a stricter mode-specific policy is configured; differences are visible in safe status**
**Given** both Automatic Response Mode and Confirmation Response Mode are available
**When** Content Safety Policy is evaluated during setup
**Then** both modes use the same active policy unless a stricter mode-specific policy is configured
**And** mode-specific differences are visible in safe setup status.

**AC4 - The full activation gate is complete: all gates valid → `hexa` is active and callable; any uncertain dependency blocks**
**Given** Agent readiness is evaluated
**When** identity, Provider/model, response mode, approver policy where applicable, lifecycle, and Content Safety Policy are all valid
**Then** `hexa` can be marked active and callable in setup readiness
**And** any missing, stale, ambiguous, disabled, or unavailable dependency blocks activation.

## Dev Notes

### Critical Guardrails

- This story is **Content Safety Policy configuration onto the existing `Agent` aggregate + the content-safety activation gate (the final Epic 1 readiness gate), ONLY**. Do NOT implement: **safety enforcement** — applying the policy to generated content so unsafe output cannot post or become an approvable proposal (FR-27 is **Epic 2 / Story 2.4**, with approver-override enforcement in **Story 3.5**); the FrontComposer setup UI / readiness badge rendering (1.8); audit-evidence query surfacing of the safety decision (FR-24, **Story 4.2**); launch-readiness metric/latency/cost gates (FR-28, **Epic 4**); the concrete safety-filter/content-policy provider and the exact category/filter taxonomy (deferred — PRD OQ-9); the live read-model binding; or any live command-dispatch / AppHost topology wiring. The aggregate makes **no dependency reads** in this story — content safety is self-contained Agent configuration, so it needs **no resolver port, no verdict evaluator, and no trusted envelope extension** (this is *simpler* than the 1.5 provider and 1.6 approver gates). [Source: epics.md#Epic-1; prd.md#4-10-content-safety-and-launch-readiness; ARCHITECTURE-SPINE.md#Deferred (Safety filters/content policy provider); prd.md#12-open-questions (OQ-9)]
- **Configuration-time vs enforcement-time split (the central scope line).** 1.7 *defines and stores* the Content Safety Policy (prompt constraints, blocked/restricted categories, failure handling, audit treatment, policy version, optional mode overrides) and *gates activation* on a policy being present and valid. Epic 2 *applies* the policy to generated content before any Conversation side effect (Story 2.4), and Epic 3 *enforces* the override path at approval time (Story 3.5). Store here; enforce there. [Source: prd.md#4-10 (FR-26 store, FR-27 enforce); epics.md#Story-1.7; #Story-2.4-generate-and-safety-check-agent-output; #Story-3.5]
- **The `Agent` aggregate owns the Content Safety Policy (AD-2).** Layer it onto the **existing** `Agent` aggregate (1.3–1.6) — extend it; do not create a new aggregate, and do not fold it onto `ProviderCatalog`/`AgentInteraction`. The interaction snapshot (Epic 2) will copy the `ContentSafetyPolicyVersion` at request time (AD-4), which is exactly why a policy change bumps both `ContentSafetyPolicyVersion` and `ConfigurationVersion`. [Source: ARCHITECTURE-SPINE.md#AD-2-Aggregate-Boundaries; #AD-4-Interaction-Snapshot]
- **Aggregates stay pure (AD-3) — and content safety is the purest gate yet.** The policy is entirely self-contained Agent state; there is no Tenants/Parties/Conversations/provider read to perform, so there is **no trusted `…:validation` envelope extension** for content safety. The activation gate reads `state.ContentSafety is not null` directly. Because an empty/invalid policy is rejected at configuration time, "policy present" ≡ "policy valid," and a direct-gateway `ActivateAgent` **cannot bypass the gate** (there is no verdict to omit/forge). The aggregate emits events only — no I/O, no `UtcNow`, no `Guid.NewGuid`. [Source: ARCHITECTURE-SPINE.md#AD-3-Pure-Aggregates-Side-Effects-Outside; #Consistency-Conventions (Mutation, Time)]
- **Fail closed on content-safety readiness (AD-12, AC2/AC4).** No active Content Safety Policy → activation is rejected with `MissingContentSafetyPolicy`; the rejection and status never treat a missing policy as permissive and never echo policy content. This completes the AC4 capstone: with this gate, the full Epic 1 readiness set (display name, instructions, party identity, provider/model, response mode, approver policy where applicable, content safety) must all be valid before `hexa` is `Active` and callable; any missing/stale/ambiguous/disabled/unavailable dependency blocks activation. [Source: ARCHITECTURE-SPINE.md#AD-12-Authorization-And-Dependency-Uncertainty; epics.md#Story-1.7 AC2/AC4; EXPERIENCE.md#uj-1 (climax: "hexa becomes active and callable only after every required gate is valid")]
- **Policy changes affect future Agent Calls only; nothing is rewritten (AC1, AD-4).** A changed policy emits a new append-only event and bumps the version(s); prior events are immutable. `AgentInteraction` (Epic 2) snapshots the content-safety policy version at request time, so in-flight/historical interactions are unaffected. Re-applying an equal configuration is a deterministic `NoOp` (AD-13). [Source: ARCHITECTURE-SPINE.md#AD-4; #AD-13-Idempotent-External-Effects; prd.md#4-10 (FR-26 "changes are auditable and affect future Agent Calls only"); epics.md#Story-1.7 AC1]
- **Both modes share the active policy unless a stricter mode-specific policy is configured (AC3).** The `ActivePolicy` is the default for both Automatic and Confirmation; optional `AutomaticModePolicy`/`ConfirmationModePolicy` are stricter overrides. The status view surfaces *which* modes carry an override (`HasAutomaticContentSafetyOverride`/`HasConfirmationContentSafetyOverride`) so the 1.8 setup status can show mode-specific differences **without exposing policy content** (AC2/AC3). Machine-proving that an override is genuinely stricter is deferred with the safety-filter provider (OQ-9); V1 records the override as the effective policy for that mode and treats the configurer as responsible for keeping it at least as restrictive. [Source: prd.md#4-10 (FR-26 mode-specific policy); epics.md#Story-1.7 AC3]
- **Authorization + tenant isolation fail closed before mutation/disclosure (AD-12, FR-19/20).** Reuse the transitional, server-populated `actor:agentsAdmin` extension gate exactly as 1.3–1.6 — client-supplied reserved extensions (`actor:agentsAdmin`, `provider:selectionValidation`, `approver:policyValidation`) are stripped at the entry point and never trusted. Unauthorized config → `AgentAdministrationDeniedRejection`. "Release operator" (AC1/AC2) is, in V1, folded under this single admin gate; the distinct release-operator role is introduced with the production authorization model / FR-28 launch-readiness work (Epic 4) — do not add a second transitional key here. Config changes are auditable (the durable event is the audit evidence — FR-24). [Source: prd.md#4-7 (FR-19/FR-20/FR-24); prd.md#4-10 (FR-26 release operator); ARCHITECTURE-SPINE.md#AD-12; 1-6-configure-response-mode-and-approver-policy.md#Critical-Guardrails]

### Design: Where Each Responsibility Lives (no AD-3 round-trip needed)

```
Admin/Release-operator/API → Server orchestration (Application/Agents) ── authorize actor (agentsAdmin)
   CONFIG PATH (store only — self-contained, NO dependency reads, NO verdict):
      AgentContentSafetyPolicyOrchestrator → dispatch ConfigureAgentContentSafetyPolicy(Configuration)
            envelope.Extensions (server-populated, client-stripped): actor:agentsAdmin = true
                  │
   pure AgentAggregate.Handle ────────────┘  structural validation + normalization only → emits
        AgentContentSafetyPolicyConfigured(Configuration, ContentSafetyPolicyVersion, ConfigurationVersion)

   ACTIVATION PATH (the content-safety gate is a PURE STATE CHECK — no resolver, no verdict):
      AgentActivationProviderRevalidation   (UNCHANGED — still re-resolves provider + approver only)
            └─ dispatch ActivateAgent
                  envelope.Extensions: actor:agentsAdmin, provider:selectionValidation, approver:policyValidation
                        │
   pure AgentAggregate.Handle(ActivateAgent) ─┘  ComputeActivationBlockers(... hasContentSafetyPolicy: state.ContentSafety is not null)
```

Why no trusted envelope extension (unlike 1.4 `party:linkValidation` / 1.5 `provider:selectionValidation` / 1.6 `approver:policyValidation`): those gates depend on *external* state (Parties / catalog / Tenants+Conversations) that the pure aggregate cannot read, so the server resolves them and feeds a trusted verdict the aggregate independently fails closed on. Content safety is *internal* Agent configuration — the aggregate already holds it in rehydrated state. There is nothing to resolve and nothing to spoof; the gate reads `state.ContentSafety is not null` directly. This makes the content-safety gate strictly *stronger* than a verdict gate (it can never be bypassed by omitting a verdict on a direct command). [Source: ARCHITECTURE-SPINE.md#AD-3; #AD-12; 1-6-configure-response-mode-and-approver-policy.md#Design-Where-Each-Responsibility-Lives]

No resolver port, no verdict evaluator, and no trusted envelope extension were added; the activation gate is a pure `state.ContentSafety is not null` check, so a direct-gateway `ActivateAgent` cannot bypass it (no verdict to omit/forge). `AgentActivationProviderRevalidation` is untouched.
- **Validation/normalization (AC1):** `ValidateAndNormalizeContentSafety` requires a non-null config + active policy; `ValidateAndNormalizePolicy` rejects `Unknown` failure-handling/audit-treatment sentinels, trims + blank-drops + ordinally de-duplicates each descriptor/category list (explicit trim because `NormalizeOptionalText` does not trim), and requires ≥1 constraint or category so an empty policy can never satisfy the gate. Each configured mode override must itself be a valid policy. Reasons are safe classifications — never echo configured content (AD-14).
- **Idempotency (AD-13):** a re-asserted equal configuration is a `NoOp` via an explicit by-value compare (`SequenceEqual` on the string lists + equal enums + matching null/non-null overrides), since record equality does not deep-compare the list members. A genuine change bumps both `ContentSafetyPolicyVersion` (AC1) and `ConfigurationVersion` (AD-4), future-only.
- **Sensitive-content (AC2; AD-14):** policy content (prompt constraints, blocked/restricted categories) lives only on the durable event + state; the compact `AgentStatusView` exposes only `HasContentSafetyPolicy`, `ContentSafetyPolicyVersion`, and the two mode-override booleans (AC3) — never the lists. The Story 1.1 secret/PII guards stay green and unweakened.
- **Activation gate ordering (AC4 capstone):** `MissingContentSafetyPolicy = 10` is appended last (existing ordinals preserved); deterministic order is now display name → instructions → party identity → provider selection → provider unavailable → response mode → approver policy missing → approver policy unresolvable → content safety policy missing.
- **Regression updates (as 1.5/1.6 did):** `StateWithSelectedProvider` and `StateConfirmationReady` now also record a content-safety policy (each terminal `ConfigurationVersion` +1, `ContentSafetyPolicyVersion = 1`); exact-blocker-list assertions for policy-less states gained `MissingContentSafetyPolicy` last; the E2E journeys (`AgentLifecycleE2ETests`) gained a `ConfigureAgentContentSafetyPolicy` step and shifted `ConfigurationVersion` assertions (disable journey 4→5, replay journey 5→6).
- **Authorization:** the "release operator" actor (AC1/AC2) is, in V1, folded under the same transitional, server-populated `actor:agentsAdmin` gate every other config command uses; the distinct release-operator role is documented as Epic 4 work. No second transitional extension key was introduced.
